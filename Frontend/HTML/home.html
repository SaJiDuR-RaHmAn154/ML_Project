ECG & Heart Monitoring

Electrocardiogram (ECG) helps detect arrhythmias and heart disease.

Key Cardiac Terms
  • Myocardial Infarction: Commonly known as a heart attack, occurs when blood flow to the heart is blocked.
  • Arrhythmia: Irregular heartbeat, can be too fast, too slow, or erratic.
  • Angina: Chest pain due to reduced blood flow to the heart muscles.
  • Cholesterol: Fatty substance in blood; high levels increase heart disease risk.
  • Hypertension: High blood pressure, a major risk factor for heart disease.

Prevention Tips
  • Eat a balanced, low-salt, low-fat diet rich in fruits and vegetables.
  • Exercise regularly (at least 30 min/day, 5 days a week).
  • Avoid tobacco and limit alcohol consumption.
  • Get 7-8 hours of quality sleep each night.
  • Monitor your blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar regularly.
  • Maintain a healthy weight and waistline.
  • Stay hydrated and avoid sugary drinks.
