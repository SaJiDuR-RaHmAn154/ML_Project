Heart Disease Prevention

Take control of your heart health with evidence-based prevention strategies. 80% of premature heart disease is preventable through lifestyle changes.

Check Your Risk Learn More

Essential Prevention Steps

Follow these proven strategies to maintain a healthy heart

1

Eat Heart-Healthy Foods

  • Fruits and vegetables daily
  • Whole grains over refined
  • Lean proteins (fish, poultry)
  • Low-fat dairy products
  • Limit saturated fats
  • Reduce sodium intake
2

Stay Physically Active

  • 150 minutes moderate exercise
  • 75 minutes vigorous exercise
  • Strength training 2+ days
  • Daily walking routine
  • Take stairs over elevators
  • Find enjoyable activities
3

Maintain Healthy Weight

  • Calculate your BMI regularly
  • Aim for BMI 18.5-24.9
  • Monitor waist circumference
  • Set realistic weight goals
  • Track your progress weekly
  • Consult healthcare provider
4

Avoid Tobacco & Limit Alcohol

  • No smoking or tobacco use
  • Avoid secondhand smoke
  • Seek help to quit smoking
  • Moderate alcohol consumption
  • Women: ≤1 drink per day
  • Men: ≤2 drinks per day
5

Get Quality Sleep

  • 7-8 hours sleep per night
  • Consistent sleep schedule
  • Create calming bedtime routine
  • Limit screen time before bed
  • Keep bedroom cool & dark
  • Address sleep disorders promptly
6

Monitor Your Health

  • Regular blood pressure checks
  • Monitor cholesterol levels
  • Check blood sugar levels
  • Schedule annual physical exams
  • Follow medication regimens
  • Know your family history

Manage Stress Effectively

Chronic stress can contribute to heart disease. Learn healthy ways to cope with stress.

Practice Meditation
Stay Connected
Enjoy Hobbies
